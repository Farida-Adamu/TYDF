About the Organisation

The TY Danjuma Foundation is a private philanthropic foundation established in 2009 by General Theophilus Yakubu Danjuma (rtd) and his wife Daisy Danjuma. The Foundation is committed to improving the well-being of vulnerable and marginalised people in Nigeria, with a focus on healthcare, education, and peace-building across underserved communities.

Operating primarily in the South South and North Central zones, the Foundation works through strategic partnerships with civil society organisations, government bodies, and international development agencies to achieve systemic, lasting change. The Foundation is particularly recognised for its grassroots health systems strengthening work and its scholarship programmes for young Nigerians from low-income families.

Active Programmes

Community Health Systems Strengthening Active

Healthcare · South South

A multi-year initiative to improve primary healthcare delivery in six states across Nigeria's South South zone, including supply chain strengthening, community health worker training, and infrastructure upgrades.

TYD Scholarship Programme Active

Education · National

Full tertiary scholarships for high-achieving students from low-income households across Nigeria. 450+ scholars supported since inception across 28 Nigerian universities.

North Central Peace & Reconciliation Initiative Completed

Peace-Building · North Central

A two-year community mediation and conflict resolution programme operating in Plateau, Benue, and Nasarawa states. Programme closed December 2024 with full evaluation report available.
